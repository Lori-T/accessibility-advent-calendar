The International Day of Persons with Disabilities (IDPD) is observed annually on December 3rd. The theme for this year is "Amplifying the leadership of persons with disabilities for an inclusive and sustainable future." This theme recognises the important role that persons with disabilities play in creating a more inclusive and sustainable world for all. It also emphasises the importance of the participation of persons with disabilities in decision-making processes that affect their lives.

What is a disability?

"A person has a disability […] if they have a physical or mental impairment and the impairment has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on their ability to carry out normal day-to-day activities” - Equality Act 2010 Approximately 1 in 4 people (24%) in the UK have a disability or long-term impairment. These stats do not include unreported disabilities, neurodiversity (such as Autism or ADHD) or situational (for example, a new parent holding a baby) or temporary impairments (for example, a person with an arm injury)
